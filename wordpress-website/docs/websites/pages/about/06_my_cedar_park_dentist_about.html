Routine Dental Care in Cedar Park

At our Cedar Park, TX, dental office, we offer a wide range of routine dental treatments to help keep your smile healthy. From cleanings and exams to more advanced care, we’re here to meet all your dental needs.

Root Canal Treatment

A root canal is used to save a tooth that has become infected or damaged. During this treatment, we remove the infected tissue inside the tooth, clean it out, and then seal it to prevent further infection. Root canals help save your natural teeth, so you don’t need an extraction. Most patients feel little to no pain during the procedure, and it can prevent further tooth problems in the future.

Tooth Extraction

If a tooth is too damaged to be saved, we may recommend a tooth extraction. Extractions are done when a tooth is badly decayed, broken, or causing problems like crowding. We make sure the process is as comfortable as possible. Afterward, we can discuss options like dental implants or bridges to replace the missing tooth and restore your smile.

Oral Surgery

We offer dental extractions, dental implants, and other oral surgery methods to help patients with various oral health problems. These surgeries may involve treating impacted teeth or performing bone grafting to prepare for future implant placement.

Our office uses anesthesia and dental sedation to ensure comfort during any oral surgery procedure. Whether you need local anesthesia to numb the area or deeper sedation to help you relax, we tailor the sedation method to your specific needs. We aim to ensure your surgery and healing process go as smoothly and pain-free as possible.

Digital Dentistry

With dental technology, we can treat almost all dental health needs in one convenient location. Our office uses digital X-rays to see dental problems we cannot see during a visual examination. Intraoral scans also allow us to take impressions of the smile without discomfort.

Traditional dental impressions use bitter-tasting, messy putty. However, intraoral scans do not require any putty. We move the scanner over the teeth to get an instant 3-D view of your smile.
