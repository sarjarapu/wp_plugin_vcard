Redirect any cost questions toward consultations.

## Content Analysis
### Writing Style & Tone
- Conversational, second-person language dominates ("you", "your smile"), paired with first-person plural assurances ("we guide you").
- Sentences are generally 15–22 words with supportive subheads for scannability. Long sections often break into bullet lists for benefits, treatment steps, or FAQs.
- Trust levers: dentist bios, before/after claims (without photos), review counts, and technology mentions.
- Bilingual or inclusive copy (English + Spanish) appears on My Dental Salon and should inspire at least one bilingual reassurance block for Aviva’s diverse patients.

### Section Patterns (frequency observed)
1. **Hero** with subheading + CTA (13/13)
2. **What Invisalign Is / How it Works** (13/13)
3. **Benefits vs. Braces** (11/13)
4. **Who’s a Candidate / Candidacy checklist** (9/13)
5. **Treatment Process / Timeline** (9/13)
6. **Technology & Materials** (8/13; scanners, SmartTrack, attachments)
7. **Lifestyle/Tips (care, eating, retainers)** (7/13)
8. **FAQs** (11/13) and often 5–8 questions
9. **Why Choose [Practice]** (12/13) with office differentiators
10. **Financing / Insurance / Special Offers CTA** (8/13)

### CTA Analysis
- Successful pages place a CTA in the hero, after the benefits/process, near FAQs, and as a final block with phone + form link.
- Language focuses on immediacy: "Schedule Online", "Book Your Free Consultation", "Call to start". Swish and Prime use dual buttons (call + form) stacked.
- Phone numbers are clickable; some pair with hours or "text us" instructions.
- Recommended for Aviva: at least four CTA placements (hero, mid-page after technology/benefits, after FAQs, final lead-capture) plus inline phone references.

### SEO Analysis
- Title tags stay within 55–60 characters and always combine service + city ("Invisalign Clear Aligners in Cedar Park, TX | Practice").
- Meta descriptions (150–160 chars) emphasize discreet orthodontics, comfort, and CTA.
- Structured data varies: some use `Dentist`, others `MedicalProcedure`. Few include detailed schema, so Aviva can stand out with a `MedicalProcedure` snippet nested under Aviva’s existing `Dentist` schema.
- Keyword coverage beyond "Invisalign": "clear aligners Cedar Park", "invisible braces", "Invisalign Teen", "SmartTrack technology", "digital impressions".
- Internal links typically go to contact pages, financing info, or smile galleries.

### Technology & Features
- Frequent mentions: iTero® digital scanning, SmartTrack material, SmartForce attachments, remote monitoring apps, Vivera retainers, whitening add-ons, and same-day emergency availability.
- Patients respond to pain/comfort cues (no wires, removable, easier hygiene) and speed (average 6–18 months) without promising exact timelines.
- Several practices highlight flexible scheduling, virtual consults, or multi-location coverage.

7. **What to Expect During Treatment:** Set expectations for aligner wear (20–22 hours/day), switching cadence, attachments, handling refinements, and how Aviva supports compliance (text reminders, same-day emergency help, bilingual team assistance).

9. **Aftercare & Retainer Guidance:** Outline retention, cleaning, and long-term maintenance (Vivera/TBD) plus tie to Aviva’s preventive visits and membership plan/care credit options (no pricing, just mention support).
10. **FAQs:** 6–8 refreshed Q&As covering treatment length, comfort, attachments, eating/drinking, insurance, and how Aviva handles emergencies/remote travel.
11. **Why Aviva / Social Proof:** Reiterate 4.9★/297 reviews, local ownership, same-day emergency policy, HIPAA/privacy safeguards, amenities (music, warm towels, kids’ area). Add quote or micro-testimonial snippet referencing home/about voice.

## Implementation Guidelines
- **Content type:** Educational + persuasive; keep paragraphs 2–4 sentences with supportive bullet lists for benefits, candidacy, and care tips.
- **Length:** Target 1,150–1,300 words to stay competitive with Prime, Swish, and Park Family Dental while remaining scannable.
- **Tone:** Friendly, confident, inclusive (English-first with a Spanish reassurance block). Use first-person plural and direct second-person address.
- **CTA placement:** Hero, after "Technology & Expertise", after FAQs, and final callout. Include phone + scheduling link each time.
- **Internal links:** Link to `/book-appointment/`, `/virtual-tour/`, and relevant blog/amenities pages where natural.
- **Schema:** Add `MedicalProcedure` JSON-LD referencing Invisalign, Aviva Dental Care as provider, location, phone, and same-day emergency availability.
- **Pricing handling:** Remove explicit costs. Use language such as "Every smile is unique—schedule a consultation for a personalized aligner plan and cost estimate. Our team reviews insurance benefits, Aviva Wellness Plan perks, and financing options with you."
- **SEO specifics:**
  - Title: `Invisalign Clear Aligners in Cedar Park, TX | Aviva Dental Care`
  - Meta: `Straighten your smile discreetly with Invisalign at Aviva Dental Care in Cedar Park, TX. Digital impressions, personalized plans, and comfort-first visits—schedule your consultation.`
  - H1 repeats service + location.
  - Use secondary keywords throughout: "clear aligners", "invisible braces", "Invisalign Teen", "digital impressions", "aligner aftercare".
- **Accessibility & style:** Follow home/about spacing, `.container` wrappers, `.section-intro` & `.section-text` classes, `.cta-button` styles, and maintain image alt attributes if images are added later.

This strategy ensures the final Invisalign page delivers the same polished layout as the refreshed home/about pages, integrates proven competitor tactics, features Aviva-specific trust markers, and keeps every CTA laser-focused on booking a consultation instead of discussing price.
