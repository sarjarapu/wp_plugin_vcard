## Content Analysis
### Writing Style & Tone
- Competitors balance educational and persuasive tones, often using empathetic language (“relief,” “comfort,” “save your tooth”) and plain-language explanations.
- Park Family, Whitestone, and Parmer Oaks include data (success rates, timelines) to boost credibility, while Bowcutt and Bell Blvd use myth-busting language to reframe anxiety.
- My Dental Salon’s bilingual copy demonstrates trust with Spanish-speaking audiences—a differentiator Aviva should echo with a concise Spanish reassurance block.

### Section Patterns
Top recurring sections:
1. **Hero** with H1, summary, phone number, and “Schedule consultation” CTA.
2. **Symptoms / When you need a root canal** (Bullets of pain, sensitivity, swelling, gum pimples).
3. **Benefits** (pain relief, preserving natural tooth, preventing spread, protecting smile).
4. **Process walkthrough** (diagnostics → anesthesia → cleaning → sealing → crown) often as numbered steps or infographic-style columns.
5. **Comfort & Technology** (sedation, CBCT scans, apex locators, digital imaging, spa-like amenities).
6. **Aftercare & Recovery timeline** (24-hour, 2–3 day, crown follow-up) plus prevention tips.
7. **FAQs** answering pain, duration, insurance/cost, alternatives, and when to call.
8. **Trust panels** (testimonials, success stats, doctor bio, emergency availability) and location-specific info.
9. **Final CTA / contact band** with phone + form.

### CTA Analysis
- Every page repeats CTAs 3+ times: hero button + phone, sticky nav phone, mid-page contact card, and footer CTA. Many include phone callouts embedded within text (e.g., “Call (512) 250-2356”).
- Language trends: “Schedule Online,” “Call Now,” “Book Your Consultation,” “Request An Appointment,” often paired with urgency (“Don’t wait for the pain to worsen”).
- Best practice: hero CTA (button + phone), mid-page comfort/diagnostics CTA, bilingual CTA for Spanish readers, and final lead-capture block referencing both phone `(512) 852-8528` and contact form.

### SEO Analysis
- Titles pair service + location + brand (e.g., “Root Canal Treatment in Cedar Park, TX | Practice Name”).
- Meta descriptions include primary keyword + benefit + CTA (~155 chars).
- Structured data varies: some include `MedicalProcedure` schema; Park Family adds FAQ schema.
- Keyword usage: “root canal Cedar Park,” “endodontic therapy,” “save natural tooth,” “emergency root canal,” “gentle root canal treatment.”
- Internal links: better pages link to emergency dentistry, crowns, financing, and about pages.
- Recommendation: implement `MedicalProcedure` schema referencing Dr. Apurva, include internal links to emergency dentistry, dental crowns, and virtual tour pages already in Aviva’s site map.

### Technology & Features
- Park Family + Prime Dental spotlight CBCT, AI-enhanced digital X-rays, apex locators, microscopes, STA Single Tooth Anesthesia—positioning technology as comfort + precision.
- Whitestone and Cedar Park Modern highlight sedation, same-day relief, and flexible financing.
- Takeaway: Aviva should leverage its “Modern Facility & Advanced Technology” messaging from home/about pages—digital X-rays, 3D imaging, intraoral cameras—and tie it directly to root canal accuracy and comfort. Also surface Aviva’s comfort amenities (heated blankets, aromatherapy towels, noise-canceling headphones) to differentiate.
