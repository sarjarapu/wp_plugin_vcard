Healthy Smiles Start with Proactive Dental Protection

At Mosaic Dentistry, we believe the best dental treatment is the one you never need. Our preventative care services are designed to help patients of all ages avoid common oral health issues like tooth decay, gum disease, and enamel erosion before they start. Through regular cleanings, screenings, and personalized education, we provide the foundation for strong, healthy smiles that last a lifetime.

Serving Cedar Park and surrounding areas—including Leander, Round Rock, Jonestown, Austin, and Lago Vista—we offer modern, compassionate preventative dentistry tailored to your unique needs.

Why Preventative Dental Care Matters

Preventative care isn’t just about clean teeth—it’s about preserving your oral health and reducing your risk of complex dental problems down the road. Small problems like plaque buildup or early-stage decay can quickly turn into larger concerns if not addressed early.

Our approach to preventative dentistry focuses on:

  • Identifying problems before they become painful or costly
  • Promoting long-term oral health through education and routine care
  • Reducing your risk of cavities, gum disease, enamel wear, and tooth loss
  • Supporting whole-body health by managing oral-systemic links (such as diabetes and heart disease)

Preventative care can save you time, money, and discomfort—while keeping your smile looking its best.

What's Included in Our Preventative Dental Services

At Mosaic Dentistry, our preventative care includes a full suite of services designed to protect your smile at every stage of life.

Comprehensive Dental Exams

We perform thorough evaluations of your teeth, gums, bite, and soft tissues to catch early signs of decay, gum disease, or oral abnormalities. Exams also include oral cancer screenings to monitor for changes that may require further attention.

Professional Dental Cleanings

Our hygienists remove plaque and tartar buildup that brushing and flossing can’t reach. Regular cleanings help prevent cavities, gingivitis, and bad breath.

Digital X-Rays

We use low-radiation digital imaging to detect problems that may not be visible during an exam, such as bone loss, hidden decay, or infections below the surface.

Fluoride Treatments

Fluoride strengthens enamel and reduces the risk of cavities—especially beneficial for children, teens, and patients with a history of decay.

Dental Sealants

For children and cavity-prone adults, sealants protect the deep grooves of molars from bacteria and food particles, dramatically reducing cavity risk.

Personalized Oral Hygiene Guidance

We provide brushing and flossing tips based on your specific needs and recommend tools that make your at-home care more effective.

Who Needs Preventative Dentistry?

Everyone can benefit from regular preventative dental care—from toddlers getting their first tooth to seniors maintaining their oral health in later years.

Preventative care is especially important for:

  • Children and teens (developing teeth are more cavity-prone)
  • Adults with a history of dental decay or gum disease
  • Patients with chronic conditions like diabetes or dry mouth
  • Individuals with braces, implants, or dental restorations
  • Anyone who wants to avoid costly or invasive treatment later

By tailoring care to your age, oral health history, and lifestyle, we help you prevent problems before they begin.

How Often Should You See the Dentist?

Most patients benefit from a preventative care visit every six months, which typically includes a cleaning and exam. However, we may recommend more frequent visits if you:

  • Are prone to cavities or gum disease
  • Have dental restorations such as crowns or implants
  • Smoke or use tobacco products
  • Are undergoing orthodontic treatment
  • Have underlying health conditions that affect your oral health

During your appointment, we’ll build a custom care schedule to support your individual needs.

Preventative Care vs. Restorative Treatment

Preventative dentistry is proactive, aiming to stop issues before they start. Restorative dentistry, on the other hand, addresses damage once it occurs. While both play a role in long-term oral health, preventative care is your first line of defense.

By investing in preventive services today, you can reduce your need for more extensive treatments like root canals, tooth extractions, or dental bridges in the future.

At Mosaic Dentistry, we create a calm and supportive experience that encourages lifelong dental habits—for both kids and adults. From your child’s first dental visit to routine care for parents and grandparents, we make it easy for families to stay healthy together.

Frequently Asked Questions About Preventative Dental Care

What’s the difference between a dental exam and a cleaning?

A dental exam is a full evaluation of your teeth, gums, and oral tissues by the dentist, while a cleaning is performed by a hygienist to remove plaque and tartar. Both are essential parts of preventative care.

Is preventative care covered by insurance?

Most dental insurance plans cover two cleanings and exams per year, along with X-rays and fluoride treatments. We’ll help you understand your benefits and make the most of your coverage.

How can I improve my preventative care at home?

Brush twice a day with fluoride toothpaste, floss daily, eat a balanced diet, and avoid tobacco. We’ll recommend products or tools to help you maintain excellent oral hygiene between visits.

When should children start preventative dental visits?

We recommend your child’s first visit by age one or within six months of their first tooth erupting. Early visits help establish a positive relationship with dental care.

Do you accept new patients for preventative care?

Yes! Whether you're new to Cedar Park or looking for a more personalized dental home, Mosaic Dentistry welcomes you and your family.
