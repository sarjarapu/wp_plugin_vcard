## Content Analysis
### Writing Style & Tone
- Conversational, second-person voice with confident assurances. Most pages balance education and persuasion, keeping paragraphs to 2–4 sentences.
- Emotional drivers: confidence boost, photo-ready smiles, stain frustrations, time-saving convenience, and comfort for sensitive teeth.
- Higher-performing pages (Mosaic, Prime Dental) layer storytelling (step-by-step journey) with clinical credibility (doctor mentions, technology callouts).

### Section Patterns
Common order:
1. Hero (H1 + intro + CTA + phone)
2. Benefits/Why professional whitening (speed, safety, even results)
3. Treatment options (in-office vs. take-home vs. Zoom/Boost)
4. Process or timeline (consult → prep → whitening → maintenance)
5. Comfort & sensitivity management (desensitizers, barriers, nitrous)
6. Candidate criteria & expectation setting
7. Results longevity + aftercare tips
8. FAQs / Q&A
9. Social proof & final CTA stack (phone + form + location)

### CTA Analysis
- Average ~8 CTAs per page. Swish uses 18, Parmer 12, even shorter 512 Dentist includes 4.
- Language trends: "Book an Appointment," "Schedule Today," "Call (xxx)" and "Request an Appointment". Half pair buttons with direct phone hyperlinks; some include text/email.
- Placement: hero, after benefits, after process, within FAQ, final footer. Many combine button + phone + SMS/note.

### SEO Analysis
- Titles stay within 55–60 characters and include "Teeth Whitening" + "Cedar Park, TX".
- H1 almost always mirrors title; Cedar Park is repeated in hero copy and several H2s for local relevance.
- Average 5–6 H2s plus subheadings; top performers include structured FAQs with `Q.` labels.
- Schema: saw LocalBusiness/Dentist blocks plus occasional FAQPage or MedicalProcedure JSON-LD.
- Internal links: references to cosmetic dentistry, dental memberships, sedation pages, or financing info. External links rarely used.

### Technology & Features
- Zoom WhiteSpeed lamps, Opalescence Boost gels, custom trays, KöR deep bleaching, desensitizing pastes, and barrier isolation are recurring differentiators.
- Comfort levers: nitrous, blankets, Netflix/amenities, digital shade matching.
- Many promote membership plans or third-party financing (Cherry, CareCredit) instead of price lists.

## Implementation Guidelines
- **Length & density:** Target 1,200–1,300 words with 9–10 H2s/H3s; keep paragraphs to 2–4 sentences, following home/about spacing.
- **Voice:** First-person plural (“we”) + second-person (“you”). Confident, warm, concise.
- **SEO:**
  - Title `<title>Teeth Whitening in Cedar Park, TX | Aviva Dental Care</title>`
  - Meta description 150–160 chars referencing safety, confidence, consultation CTA.
  - H1 repeats “Teeth Whitening in Cedar Park, TX”. Include Cedar Park references in several H2s.
  - Include internal links to `/services/cosmetic-dentistry/` or other relevant Aviva pages when referencing broader care.
  - Add JSON-LD `MedicalProcedure` or extend existing LocalBusiness schema to include whitening service data.
- **Styling:** Reuse `.hero`, `.section-intro`, `.section-text`, `.contact-info`, `.cta-button` classes from home/about to stay visually consistent.
- **CTAs:** Use "Schedule your consultation", "Call (512) 852-8528", "Request an appointment". No “Book now” language linking elsewhere.
- **Media & accessibility:** Add descriptive alt text for any imagery (e.g., "Patient receiving professional teeth whitening in Cedar Park, TX"). Keep markup semantic with ordered lists for timelines and definition lists for FAQs if appropriate.
- **Regulatory:** Avoid definitive outcome promises (“guaranteed”); focus on likely results and professional oversight. No pricing, discounts, or time-limited offers.
- **Testing:** After drafting HTML, validate CTA links, ensure schema passes Rich Results test, and double-check responsive layout.
