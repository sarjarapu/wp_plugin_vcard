### Common Patterns
- **Structure:** hero + CTA → definition/benefits → candidacy → process timeline → types/materials → maintenance → FAQs → final CTA.
- **Messaging themes:** confidence boost, instant transformations, minimal downtime, enamel preservation, custom shade matching, and longevity (10–15 years) dominate copy.
- **CTA behavior:** Every page keeps a sticky phone or multi-placement button. Verbs include "Schedule Online," "Book Consultation," "Call Today," and "Reserve Appointment." Mid-page CTAs often follow benefits or process sections.
- **Trust markers:** Mention of technology (CEREC, CBCT, digital impressions), sedation dentistry menus, testimonial quotes, review counts, and membership/plan language.
- **Bilingual cues:** New Hope, Parmer Oaks, and My Dental weave Spanish subheads or duplicate FAQs—a clear opportunity to show inclusivity.

### Gaps & Opportunities
- Sparse mention of digital smile design mockups, same-day temp protocols, or communication touches (text updates, follow-up calls); Aviva can own this.
- Limited discussion of comfort amenities beyond "gentle" phrasing; Aviva’s aromatherapy towels, noise-canceling headphones, and sedation partners are differentiators.
- Few pages tie veneers into comprehensive smile plans (orthodontics, whitening prep). Cross-linking to Invisalign/whitening can guide multi-service cases.

## Content Analysis
### Writing Style & Tone
- Conversational, second person (“you/your”) with supportive, confident language.
- Paragraphs average 2–4 sentences (≈90–120 words). Lists break up dense info for benefits, steps, or aftercare tips.
- Tone balances education (definitions, candidacy criteria) with persuasion (before/after promises, social proof). Many pages echo spa/concierge language when referencing comfort.

### Section Patterns (Recommended order)
1. Hero with service promise + buttons.
2. What veneers are + problems solved.
3. Benefits & candidacy checklist (chips, discoloration, gaps, short teeth).
4. Veneer types (porcelain vs. composite vs. no-prep) + when each fits.
5. Process breakdown (consult → digital design → prep → temporaries → delivery) including timeline + comfort touches.
6. Technology & materials (digital impressions, CBCT evaluation, lab partners, quality controls).
7. Results & longevity expectations + maintenance tips.

9. FAQs/People-also-ask (pain, candidacy, insurance, lifespan, repairs, alternatives).
10. Why choose Aviva / differentiators + final CTA with phone + form.

## Pricing Information Handling
- **Rule:** Do not list costs or ranges. Emphasize personalized treatment planning.
- **Recommended language:**
  - "Veneer investments vary based on the number of teeth, materials, and whether we pair them with whitening or Invisalign. We review transparent estimates, insurance benefits, and financing at your consultation."
  - "Nuestro equipo explica cada opción de pago en persona para que el plan se ajuste a tu presupuesto." (Spanish reassurance)
  - Highlight acceptance of PPO plans, in-house membership, CareCredit/financing, and bundling options without quoting numbers.
- **CTA tie-in:** Follow pricing language with "Schedule Your Consultation" button + phone number to keep focus on booking rather than cost shopping.
